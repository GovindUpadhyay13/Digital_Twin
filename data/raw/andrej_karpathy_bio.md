# Andrej Karpathy

Andrej Karpathy (born October 23, 1986) is a Slovak-Canadian computer scientist, AI researcher, and educator recognized for shaping how modern neural-network practitioners learn and build systems. He co-founded OpenAI, led Autopilot vision at Tesla, founded Eureka Labs, and created the widely followed “Neural Networks: Zero to Hero” course. In 2026 he joined Anthropic to advance pre-training research on its Claude models.

## Key facts
- Born: October 23, 1986 in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia
- Education: B.Sc. University of Toronto; M.Sc. University of British Columbia; PhD Stanford University (2015) under Fei-Fei Li
- Notable roles: Founding research scientist at OpenAI (2015–17); Director of AI at Tesla (2017–22); Founder of Eureka Labs (2024 – ); Joined Anthropic (2026)
- Known for: AI education (CS231n, Zero to Hero), open-source tools (micrograd, nanoGPT), and the term “vibe coding.”
- Honors: TIME 100 AI (2024); Collins Dictionary Word of the Year (2025, “vibe coding”).

## Academic and industry trajectory
Karpathy’s Stanford PhD merged computer vision and natural language processing, yielding influential papers like Deep Visual-Semantic Alignments (CVPR 2015). At OpenAI he worked on early reinforcement-learning and generative-model experiments. Recruited to Tesla in 2017, he led the team training neural networks for camera-only Autopilot perception and shared technical architectures publicly at Tesla AI Day 2021.

## Educational influence
Karpathy’s teaching style emphasizes code-level intuition. The Stanford course CS231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition and his YouTube series Neural Networks: Zero to Hero introduced deep learning fundamentals to millions worldwide. His open-source repositories—micrograd, minGPT, nanoGPT—serve as minimal, readable frameworks for understanding backpropagation and transformer models.

## Recent work and concepts
In 2024 he founded Eureka Labs, an AI-native education startup developing LLM-driven courses such as LLM101n. He later joined Anthropic’s Claude pre-training team to apply AI methods to accelerate model research. Karpathy also coined the concept of “Software 2.0”—training networks instead of hand-coding logic—and later extended it to “Software 3.0,” where natural-language prompts become the program.

## Impact
Karpathy bridges research labs and the developer community, popularizing deep learning through transparent education and open code. His career—from OpenAI’s founding to Anthropic’s autoresearch projects—mirrors AI’s evolution from academic exploration to recursive self-improvement at the frontier.
